# Thailand MoPH Prescription Rules

## Key Legal Framework

1. **Drug Act B.E. 2510 (1967)** (พ.ร.บ. ยา พ.ศ. 2510) - Primary drug regulation law
2. **Narcotics Code B.E. 2564** (ประมวลกฎหมายยาเสพติด พ.ศ. 2564) - Replaced the old Narcotics Act; latest amendment B.E. 2567 (2024)
3. **Psychotropic Substances Act B.E. 2518 (1975)** - Controls psychotropic medications
4. **NHSO Extended Dispensing Policy (EDP)** - Started 2016, allows 90-day prescriptions for stable chronic diseases

## Prescription Duration Limits

### Standard Outpatient Prescriptions

- **General medications**: No explicit statutory maximum in the Drug Act; hospital/insurance policy governs. Standard practice is **30 days** per prescription for most public hospitals under Universal Coverage Scheme (UCS).
- Under NHSO EDP policy (since 2016): stable chronic disease patients may receive up to **90 days** of medications per prescription, regardless of insurance scheme.
- Civil Servant Medical Benefit Scheme (CSMBS): historically allowed **90-day** prescriptions for chronic medications.
- Social Security Scheme (SSS): typically **30 days**, may extend to **90 days** for stable chronic conditions under EDP.

### Controlled Substances

- **Narcotic drugs (Category 2)** (ยาเสพติดให้โทษประเภท 2): Maximum **90 days** supply per Ministerial Regulation under Narcotics Code B.E. 2567 (2024). Category 1 (e.g., heroin) is prohibited. Category 2 includes morphine, codeine, fentanyl, methadone, etc.
- **Psychotropic substances (Categories 2, 3, 4)**: Maximum **30 days** supply per the Psychotropic Substances Act B.E. 2518. Category 1 psychotropics are prohibited.
- **Special controlled drugs** (ยาควบคุมพิเศษ): Require a licensed physician's prescription and dispensing by a pharmacist only.
- **Dangerous drugs** (ยาอันตราย): Must be dispensed by a pharmacist; no prescription required but pharmacist must assess appropriateness.
- **Household remedies** (ยาสามัญประจำบ้าน): Available over-the-counter without prescription.

### Inpatient Prescriptions

- No statutory limit on medication supply during hospitalization
- Take-home/discharge prescriptions follow outpatient rules (30-day standard, up to 90 days for EDP-eligible chronic conditions)
- Post-surgical pain medications: typically 7-14 day supply
- Antibiotics: typically course-specific (5-14 days)

## Drug Categories in Thailand

Thailand classifies drugs into 4 categories under the Drug Act B.E. 2510:

1. **ยาควบคุมพิเศษ** (Specially controlled drugs): Prescription-only, dispensed only by pharmacists
2. **ยาอันตราย** (Dangerous drugs): Dispensed by pharmacists, no prescription required
3. **ยาสามัญประจำบ้าน** (Household remedies): OTC, no pharmacist required
4. **ยาแผนโบราณ** (Traditional medicines): Regulated separately

## Prescribing Authority

- **Licensed physicians** (แพทย์): Can prescribe all drug categories
- **Licensed dentists** (ทันตแพทย์): Can prescribe drugs related to dental treatment
- **Licensed veterinarians** (สัตวแพทย์): Can prescribe drugs for animal treatment
- **Pharmacists** (เภสัชกร): Can dispense dangerous drugs without prescription; cannot prescribe specially controlled drugs independently

## Chronic Disease Extended Dispensing Policy (EDP)

### Eligible Conditions (NHSO policy since 2016)

The following NCD categories qualify for 90-day extended prescriptions when the patient's condition is stable:

- Hypertension (ความดันโลหิตสูง)
- Diabetes mellitus (เบาหวาน)
- Dyslipidemia (ไขมันในเลือดสูง)
- Chronic kidney disease (โรคไตเรื้อรัง) -- stages 1-3 only
- Heart failure (ภาวะหัวใจล้มเหลว) -- stable NYHA I-II
- Ischemic heart disease (โรคหลอดเลือดหัวใจ) -- stable, post-intervention
- Cerebrovascular disease (โรคหลอดเลือดสมอง) -- stable, post-acute phase
- COPD (โรคปอดอุดกั้นเรื้อรัง) -- stable, non-exacerbation
- Asthma (โรคหอบหืด) -- well-controlled
- Epilepsy (โรคลมชัก) -- seizure-free on stable medication
- Psychiatric disorders (โรคจิตเวช) -- stable on maintenance therapy

### Stability Criteria for EDP

- At least 2 consecutive clinic visits with controlled disease parameters
- No medication changes in the past 3 months
- No acute complications or hospitalizations in the past 6 months
- Patient demonstrates good medication adherence
