# Thailand Clinical Pathway: Chronic Kidney Disease

**Source**: Nephrology Society of Thailand (NST) Clinical Practice Guidelines for Chronic Kidney Disease 2022
**ICD-10**: N18 (Chronic Kidney Disease), N18.1-N18.5 (Stages 1-5), N18.6 (End-stage renal disease), N18.9 (CKD unspecified)
**Last Updated**: 2024

## 1. Classification / Staging

### CKD Staging by GFR (KDIGO 2012, adopted by NST)
| Stage | GFR (mL/min/1.73m2) | Description |
|-------|---------------------|-------------|
| G1 | >= 90 | Normal or high (with kidney damage markers) |
| G2 | 60-89 | Mildly decreased (with kidney damage markers) |
| G3a | 45-59 | Mildly to moderately decreased |
| G3b | 30-44 | Moderately to severely decreased |
| G4 | 15-29 | Severely decreased |
| G5 | < 15 | Kidney failure (dialysis or transplant needed) |

### Albuminuria Staging
| Category | UACR (mg/g) | Description |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| A1 | < 30 | Normal to mildly increased |
| A2 | 30-300 | Moderately increased (formerly "microalbuminuria") |
| A3 | > 300 | Severely increased (formerly "macroalbuminuria") |

### CKD Risk Prognosis (GFR + Albuminuria combined — "Heat Map")
| | A1 (< 30) | A2 (30-300) | A3 (> 300) |
|---|-----------|-------------|------------|
| G1 (>= 90) | Low risk | Moderately increased | High |
| G2 (60-89) | Low risk | Moderately increased | High |
| G3a (45-59) | Moderately increased | High | Very high |
| G3b (30-44) | High | Very high | Very high |
| G4 (15-29) | Very high | Very high | Very high |
| G5 (< 15) | Very high | Very high | Very high |

### CKD Diagnosis Criteria
- CKD is defined as abnormalities of kidney structure or function present for > 3 months:
  - eGFR < 60 mL/min/1.73m2 (confirmed on 2 occasions >= 3 months apart)
  - Albuminuria (UACR >= 30 mg/g on 2 of 3 specimens)
  - Urine sediment abnormalities (hematuria, casts)
  - Electrolyte abnormalities due to tubular disorders
  - Structural abnormalities (polycystic kidneys, reflux nephropathy)
  - History of kidney transplantation

### Common Etiologies in Thailand
- Diabetic nephropathy (leading cause, ~40% of ESKD in Thailand)
- Hypertensive nephrosclerosis (~20%)
- Chronic glomerulonephritis (IgA nephropathy common in Southeast Asia)
- Obstructive uropathy
- Chronic tubulointerstitial nephritis (herbal nephropathy — unique concern in Thai population)
- Lupus nephritis
- Polycystic kidney disease

## 2. Initial Workup

### Standard Investigations
- **Serum creatinine with eGFR** (CKD-EPI equation — NST recommends CKD-EPI without race coefficient)
- **Cystatin C-based eGFR**: if muscle mass concerns (elderly, amputees, muscle wasting) — improves accuracy
- **UACR** (urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio): spot morning urine preferred; confirm with 2 of 3 specimens
- **Urine protein-to-creatinine ratio (UPCR)**: if nephrotic-range proteinuria suspected
- Complete urinalysis with microscopy: hematuria, casts, WBC
- CBC: anemia assessment (hemoglobin, MCV, reticulocyte count)
- Serum electrolytes: Na, K, Cl, bicarbonate (metabolic acidosis)
- Calcium, phosphate, intact PTH (iPTH): CKD-MBD assessment (start from stage G3a)
- 25-hydroxyvitamin D level
- Serum albumin: nutritional status
- Fasting lipid profile
- Fasting glucose and HbA1c
- Liver function tests (AST, ALT)
- Serum uric acid
- Iron studies: serum ferritin, TSAT (iron deficiency anemia assessment)
- Hepatitis B surface antigen, Anti-HCV, Anti-HIV (pre-dialysis screening)
- Renal ultrasound: kidney size, echogenicity, obstruction, cysts, stones
- 12-lead ECG

### Conditional Investigations
- Renal biopsy: unexplained CKD, active urine sediment (dysmorphic RBCs, RBC casts), nephrotic syndrome, rapidly progressive GN, systemic disease (SLE, vasculitis)
- 24-hour urine collection: total protein, creatinine clearance, sodium excretion (dietary compliance)
- ANA, anti-dsDNA, complement (C3, C4): if lupus nephritis suspected
- ANCA (c-ANCA, p-ANCA): if vasculitis suspected
- Anti-GBM antibodies: if Goodpasture syndrome suspected
- Serum/urine protein electrophoresis (SPEP/UPEP), free light chains: if multiple myeloma suspected (especially in elderly CKD)
- Hepatitis B DNA, Hepatitis C RNA: if serology positive (quantification for treatment)
- Renal artery duplex/CT angiography: if renovascular disease suspected (asymmetric kidneys, refractory HTN, flash pulmonary edema)
- Voiding cystourethrogram (VCUG): if vesicoureteral reflux suspected
- CT KUB (non-contrast): nephrolithiasis evaluation

## 3. Treatment Protocol

### General Measures (All CKD Stages)
- **Blood pressure control**: Target < 130/80 mmHg (< 120/80 if tolerated and proteinuric)
- **Dietary counseling** (Thai-specific):
  - Protein restriction: 0.8 g/kg/day (stage G3-G4); 0.6-0.8 g/kg/day (stage G4-G5 pre-dialysis, with keto-acid supplementation if 0.6)
  - Sodium restriction: < 5 g/day (< 2 g sodium) — reduce fish sauce, fermented foods, salty snacks common in Thai diet
  - Potassium restriction: Stage G4-G5, limit high-K Thai fruits (durian, banana, coconut water, jackfruit)
  - Phosphate restriction: Stage G3b-G5, limit processed foods, cola, offal
- **Fluid management**: Individualized; 1.5-2 L/day unless edematous or oliguric
- **Exercise**: 150 min/week moderate aerobic activity (walking, swimming); adjust for functional capacity
- **Smoking cessation**: Critical — smoking accelerates CKD progression
- **Avoid nephrotoxins**: NSAIDs, herbal supplements (Ya Chud — Thai traditional mixed medications), aminoglycosides, IV contrast (minimize, use iso-osmolar with hydration protocol)

### First-line Treatment

#### RAAS Blockade (cornerstone of CKD management)
- **ACEi**: Enalapril 5-20 mg/day, Ramipril 2.5-10 mg/day
- **ARB**: Losartan 50-100 mg/day, Valsartan 80-320 mg/day, Telmisartan 40-80 mg/day, Irbesartan 150-300 mg/day
- Indication: All CKD patients with UACR >= 30 mg/g, especially diabetic nephropathy
- Monitor K and Cr: 1-2 weeks after initiation; accept up to 30% rise in creatinine (stabilization of GFR)
- Do NOT combine ACEi + ARB (ONTARGET — increased adverse events)

#### SGLT2 Inhibitor (second cornerstone — NST 2022 strongly recommends)
- **Dapagliflozin** 10 mg/day — proven benefit in CKD regardless of diabetes (DAPA-CKD trial)
- **Empagliflozin** 10 mg/day — proven benefit in CKD (EMPA-KIDNEY trial)
- Indication: eGFR >= 20 mL/min with UACR >= 200 mg/g (strongest evidence); benefit also in lower albuminuria
- Can be started at eGFR >= 20; continue even if eGFR drops below 20 unless dialysis initiated
- Expect initial "dip" in eGFR (2-5 mL/min) — this is hemodynamic and protective; do not discontinue

### Second-line Treatment

#### Non-steroidal MRA (Finerenone)
- **Finerenone** 10-20 mg/day: for diabetic kidney disease with UACR >= 30 mg/g, eGFR >= 25, K < 4.8
  - Start 10 mg/day if eGFR 25-59; 20 mg/day if eGFR >= 60
  - FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD trials: reduced kidney and cardiovascular endpoints
  - Monitor K within 1 month; hold if K > 5.5 mEq/L
- Limited availability in Thailand as of 2024; may require special access

#### GLP-1 Receptor Agonist (for diabetic CKD)
- **Semaglutide** 0.5-1.0 mg/week SC or 14 mg/day PO
- **Liraglutide** 1.2-1.8 mg/day SC
- FLOW trial (semaglutide): kidney-protective in T2DM with CKD
- Cardiovascular and kidney benefits independent of glycemic control

### CKD-Mineral and Bone Disorder (CKD-MBD) Management

| Parameter | Target (Stage G3-G5) |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Serum phosphate | 2.5-4.5 mg/dL (stage G3-G4); 3.5-5.5 mg/dL (stage G5/dialysis) |
| Serum calcium (corrected) | 8.4-10.2 mg/dL (normal range) |
| iPTH | Stage G3-G4: within normal range; Stage G5D: 2-9x upper normal (130-600 pg/mL) |
| 25-OH Vitamin D | > 30 ng/mL |

**Treatment**:
- **Phosphate binders**: Calcium carbonate 500-1500 mg with meals (if Ca normal); Sevelamer 800 mg TID with meals (if hypercalcemia or vascular calcification); Lanthanum carbonate 500-1000 mg with meals
- **Vitamin D**: Cholecalciferol 1000-4000 IU/day for deficiency; Active vitamin D (Calcitriol 0.25-0.5 mcg/day or Alfacalcidol 0.25-1 mcg/day) for secondary hyperparathyroidism with iPTH above target
- **Calcimimetic**: Cinacalcet 25-75 mg/day for secondary hyperparathyroidism refractory to vitamin D and phosphate control (mainly dialysis patients)

### Anemia Management (CKD Anemia)

| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Hemoglobin | 10-11.5 g/dL (do NOT target > 13 g/dL) |
| Ferritin | 200-500 ng/mL (dialysis); > 100 ng/mL (non-dialysis) |
| TSAT | 20-50% |

**Treatment**:
- **Iron supplementation first**: Oral ferrous fumarate 200 mg BID-TID or Ferrous sulfate 325 mg TID (stage G3-G4); IV iron (iron sucrose 200 mg or ferric carboxymaltose 500-1000 mg) if oral intolerant, dialysis patient, or TSAT < 20%
- **Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agents (ESA)**: Start when Hb < 10 g/dL, iron replete
  - Epoetin alfa 50-100 IU/kg SC 3x/week or Darbepoetin alfa 0.45 mcg/kg SC every 1-2 weeks
  - Titrate to maintain Hb 10-11.5 g/dL; reduce dose if Hb > 11.5, hold if > 13
- **HIF-PHI (Hypoxia-Inducible Factor Prolyl Hydroxylase Inhibitor)**: Roxadustat 70-100 mg PO 3x/week; Daprodustat — oral alternatives to injectable ESA; emerging in Thailand

### Metabolic Acidosis Management
- Target serum bicarbonate: >= 22 mEq/L
- **Sodium bicarbonate** 500-1000 mg PO BID-TID (or baking soda 1/2 teaspoon in water)
- Monitor sodium load; adjust if hypertension or edema worsens

### Treatment Targets Summary (NST 2022)
| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Blood pressure | < 130/80 mmHg (proteinuric); < 140/90 mmHg (non-proteinuric) |
| HbA1c (diabetic CKD) | < 7% (individualize 7-8% in advanced CKD, elderly, hypoglycemia risk) |
| LDL-C | < 100 mg/dL; < 70 mg/dL if ASCVD |
| UACR | 30% reduction from baseline (minimum goal); remission to < 30 mg/g ideal |
| Serum K | 3.5-5.0 mEq/L (< 5.5 on RAAS blockade) |
| Serum bicarbonate | >= 22 mEq/L |
| Hemoglobin | 10-11.5 g/dL |
| Phosphate | 2.5-4.5 mg/dL (pre-dialysis) |

## 4. Medications

### Expected Drug Classes
- **ACE inhibitors**: Enalapril 5-20 mg/day, Ramipril 2.5-10 mg/day, Lisinopril 5-40 mg/day
- **ARBs**: Losartan 50-100 mg/day, Valsartan 80-320 mg/day, Telmisartan 40-80 mg/day, Irbesartan 150-300 mg/day, Candesartan 8-32 mg/day
- **SGLT2 inhibitors**: Dapagliflozin 10 mg/day, Empagliflozin 10 mg/day
- **Non-steroidal MRA**: Finerenone 10-20 mg/day
- **Diuretics**: Furosemide 20-80 mg/day (stage G3-G5; thiazides less effective if eGFR < 30), Indapamide 1.5 mg/day (effective to eGFR ~15), Metolazone 2.5-5 mg/day (add-on)
- **CCBs**: Amlodipine 5-10 mg/day, Nifedipine GITS 30-60 mg/day
- **Beta-blockers**: Bisoprolol 2.5-10 mg/day (no renal adjustment), Carvedilol 12.5-50 mg/day
- **Statins**: Atorvastatin 10-80 mg/day (no renal adjustment), Rosuvastatin 5-20 mg/day (max 10 mg if eGFR < 30)
- **Phosphate binders**: Calcium carbonate 500-1500 mg with meals, Sevelamer 800-1600 mg with meals, Lanthanum carbonate 500-1000 mg with meals
- **Vitamin D**: Cholecalciferol 1000-4000 IU/day, Calcitriol 0.25-0.5 mcg/day, Alfacalcidol 0.25-1 mcg/day
- **ESAs**: Epoetin alfa 2000-10000 IU SC 1-3x/week, Darbepoetin alfa 20-100 mcg SC every 1-4 weeks
- **Iron**: Ferrous fumarate/sulfate PO, Iron sucrose 100-200 mg IV, Ferric carboxymaltose 500-1000 mg IV
- **Sodium bicarbonate**: 500-2000 mg PO BID-TID
- **Potassium binders**: Sodium polystyrene sulfonate (Kayexalate) 15-30 g PO/PR, Patiromer 8.4 g/day, Sodium zirconium cyclosilicate 5-10 g/day

### Red Flag Medications
- **NSAIDs (all types)**: AVOID — accelerate CKD progression, cause AKI, reduce RAAS inhibitor efficacy, hyperkalemia
- **Herbal supplements / Ya Chud**: STRICTLY AVOID — major cause of CKD progression and AKI in Thailand; contains unknown combinations of steroids, NSAIDs, analgesics
- **Aminoglycosides (Gentamicin, Amikacin)**: Nephrotoxic; use only if no alternative, with drug level monitoring and short courses
- **IV contrast media**: Minimize exposure; use lowest volume iso-osmolar contrast with pre/post hydration protocol if essential
- **Metformin with eGFR < 30**: Contraindicated (lactic acidosis risk); reduce dose at eGFR 30-45 (max 1000 mg/day)
- **ACEi + ARB combination**: Contraindicated — hyperkalemia and AKI risk without additional benefit
- **Lithium**: Nephrotoxic with chronic use; requires very careful monitoring if unavoidable
- **High-dose allopurinol without adjustment**: Reduce dose to 100 mg/day if eGFR < 30; Febuxostat safer alternative (no renal adjustment)
- **Gadolinium-based contrast (MRI) with eGFR < 30**: Risk of nephrogenic systemic fibrosis; use group II agents (gadobutrol) if essential
- **Potassium-sparing diuretics with eGFR < 30**: High hyperkalemia risk; use with extreme caution and frequent K monitoring

## 5. Follow-up and Monitoring

### Monitoring Schedule by CKD Stage
| Stage | Visit Frequency | Lab Frequency |
|-------|----------------|---------------|
| G1-G2, A1 | Annually | eGFR, UACR annually |
| G1-G2, A2-A3 | Every 6 months | eGFR, UACR every 6 months |
| G3a | Every 6 months | eGFR, UACR every 6 months; electrolytes, Ca/PO4/PTH annually |
| G3b | Every 3-6 months | eGFR, UACR every 3-6 months; electrolytes, CBC, Ca/PO4/PTH every 6 months |
| G4 | Every 3 months | eGFR, UACR, electrolytes, CBC every 3 months; Ca/PO4/PTH every 3-6 months; iron studies every 3-6 months |
| G5 (pre-dialysis) | Every 1-3 months | eGFR, UACR, electrolytes, CBC every 1-3 months; Ca/PO4/PTH every 1-3 months; iron studies, albumin every 3 months |
| G5D (dialysis) | Per dialysis unit protocol | Full panel monthly; Kt/V, URR monthly; PTH every 3 months |

### Additional Monitoring
| Test | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| K and Cr after RAAS/SGLT2i/MRA change | 1-2 weeks post-change |
| HbA1c (diabetic) | Every 3-6 months |
| Lipid profile | Annually; 6-12 weeks post-statin initiation |
| Hepatitis B/C serology | Annually (pre-dialysis and dialysis) |
| Nutritional assessment (albumin, BMI, dietary recall) | Every 3-6 months (stage G4-G5) |
| eGFR slope monitoring | Calculate annually; rapid decline = > 5 mL/min/year |

### Dialysis Preparation (Start at eGFR 15-20)
- Vascular access planning: AV fistula creation when eGFR < 20 (ideally 6 months before anticipated dialysis)
- Peritoneal dialysis catheter: Tenckhoff catheter placement 2-4 weeks before initiation
- Patient education: Modality choice (HD vs PD vs conservative management), transplant evaluation
- Hepatitis B vaccination: Complete series if not immune (stage G4, before dialysis)
- Pre-transplant workup: If candidate, begin at eGFR < 20

## 6. Length of Stay (if admitted)

### Admission Indications
- Acute-on-chronic kidney disease (AKI superimposed on CKD)
- Severe hyperkalemia (K > 6.5 mEq/L or ECG changes)
- Severe metabolic acidosis (pH < 7.2 or bicarbonate < 12 mEq/L)
- Uremic symptoms (encephalopathy, pericarditis, intractable nausea/vomiting)
- Fluid overload refractory to oral diuretics
- Urgent dialysis initiation (crash start)
- Renal biopsy (may be done as day case in some Thai centers)

### Expected LOS
- AKI on CKD (reversible cause identified, responds to treatment): 3-7 days
- Hyperkalemia/acidosis management: 2-4 days
- Urgent dialysis initiation: 5-10 days (includes access creation, first sessions, education)
- Planned AV fistula creation (surgical): 1-2 days
- Peritoneal dialysis catheter insertion: 2-3 days
- Renal biopsy (inpatient): 1-2 days
- Uremic complications (pericarditis, encephalopathy): 7-14 days

### Discharge Criteria
- Electrolytes stable (K < 5.5, bicarbonate > 18)
- Fluid status managed on oral diuretics
- No uremic symptoms
- If dialysis initiated: stable on modality, access functioning, patient/family trained
- Oral medications tolerated and regimen established
- Dietary counseling completed
- Follow-up appointment within 1-2 weeks (or per dialysis schedule)

## 7. Referral Criteria

- **Nephrologist** (initial referral): eGFR < 30 (stage G4); rapidly declining eGFR (> 5 mL/min/year); persistent UACR > 300 mg/g; refractory hyperkalemia or acidosis; hematuria with proteinuria (suspected glomerulonephritis); renal biopsy indication; hereditary kidney disease; CKD in pregnancy
- **Nephrologist** (early referral recommended): eGFR 30-44 with albuminuria A3; eGFR declining > 3 mL/min/year; difficult-to-manage hypertension requiring 4+ drugs
- **Vascular access surgeon**: eGFR < 20 (AV fistula planning); failed AV fistula or graft; peritoneal dialysis catheter placement
- **Transplant center**: eGFR < 20 and candidate for transplantation (age-appropriate, no absolute contraindications); living donor evaluation
- **Dietitian/Nutritionist**: All CKD stage G3-G5 patients; specialized renal diet counseling (protein, K, phosphate, Na restriction)
- **Diabetologist/Endocrinologist**: Diabetic CKD with HbA1c > 8% despite treatment; complex insulin management in advanced CKD (altered insulin clearance)
- **Cardiologist**: CKD with heart failure, uncontrolled hypertension, or cardiovascular events (CKD is coronary equivalent)
- **Urologist**: Obstructive uropathy, nephrolithiasis causing CKD, ADPKD complications
- **Palliative care / Conservative management**: Elderly with eGFR < 15 who decline or are not candidates for dialysis/transplant; symptom management and advance care planning
