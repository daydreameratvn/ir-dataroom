# Thailand Clinical Pathway: Ischemic Stroke

**Source**: Thai Stroke Network / Prasat Neurological Institute Clinical Practice Guidelines for Ischemic Stroke 2022
**ICD-10**: I63 (Cerebral Infarction), I63.0-I63.9 (subtypes), G45 (TIA)
**Last Updated**: 2024

## 1. Classification / Staging

### Ischemic Stroke Subtypes (TOAST Classification)
1. **Large artery atherosclerosis** — carotid or intracranial stenosis >= 50%
2. **Cardioembolism** — atrial fibrillation, valvular disease, recent MI, cardiomyopathy
3. **Small vessel occlusion (lacunar)** — subcortical infarct < 1.5 cm, associated with hypertension/diabetes
4. **Stroke of other determined etiology** — dissection, vasculitis, hypercoagulable states
5. **Stroke of undetermined etiology** — cryptogenic

### Severity Assessment (NIHSS — National Institutes of Health Stroke Scale)
| NIHSS Score | Severity | Clinical Implication |
|-------------|----------|---------------------|
| 0-4 | Minor stroke | Consider IV thrombolysis if disabling symptoms |
| 5-15 | Moderate stroke | IV thrombolysis indicated if within window |
| 16-20 | Moderate-severe | IV thrombolysis + consider thrombectomy |
| 21-42 | Severe stroke | IV thrombolysis + thrombectomy if LVO; ICU care |

### Bamford (Oxfordshire) Classification
- **TACI** (Total Anterior Circulation Infarct): hemiparesis + homonymous hemianopia + higher cerebral dysfunction
- **PACI** (Partial Anterior Circulation Infarct): 2 of 3 TACI components, or isolated higher cerebral dysfunction
- **LACI** (Lacunar Infarct): pure motor, pure sensory, sensorimotor, ataxic hemiparesis, dysarthria-clumsy hand
- **POCI** (Posterior Circulation Infarct): brainstem/cerebellar signs, isolated hemianopia

## 2. Initial Workup

### Hyperacute Phase (within first 60 minutes of arrival — "Door-to-Needle")
- **Non-contrast CT brain** (MANDATORY): rule out hemorrhage — target door-to-CT < 25 minutes
- **CT Angiography (CTA) head and neck**: if NIHSS >= 6, suspected large vessel occlusion (LVO), or thrombectomy candidate
- **CT Perfusion (CTP)**: if within 6-24 hour window; assess penumbra for late thrombectomy candidacy
- Point-of-care glucose (MANDATORY): rule out hypoglycemia mimicking stroke
- CBC with platelet count
- PT, aPTT, INR (do NOT delay tPA for results unless on anticoagulant)
- Serum electrolytes, creatinine
- Troponin (cardiac co-morbidity common)
- ECG (12-lead): detect atrial fibrillation

### Within 24-48 Hours (Acute Phase)
- MRI brain with DWI (diffusion-weighted imaging): confirm infarct location and extent
- MRA (MR Angiography) of intracranial and cervical vessels: if CTA not done
- Transthoracic echocardiography (TTE): all patients; assess cardiac source of embolism
- Transesophageal echocardiography (TEE): if cryptogenic stroke, suspected PFO, or intracardiac thrombus
- Carotid duplex ultrasound: if anterior circulation stroke
- Transcranial Doppler (TCD): assess intracranial stenosis, detect right-to-left shunt
- 24-72 hour cardiac telemetry or Holter monitoring: detect paroxysmal atrial fibrillation
- Fasting lipid profile
- Fasting glucose and HbA1c
- Liver function tests
- Thyroid function tests (if indicated)
- Hypercoagulability workup: if age < 55, cryptogenic, venous territory, or recurrent stroke (protein C, protein S, antithrombin III, antiphospholipid antibodies, factor V Leiden)

### Conditional Investigations
- Prolonged cardiac monitoring (14-30 days event recorder or implantable loop recorder): cryptogenic stroke with high suspicion of paroxysmal AF
- Conventional cerebral angiography (DSA): vasculitis, Moyamoya, dissection confirmation
- Lumbar puncture: suspected CNS vasculitis or infectious etiology
- ESR, CRP, ANA, anti-dsDNA: if vasculitis suspected

## 3. Treatment Protocol

### Hyperacute Treatment

#### Intravenous Thrombolysis (IV rtPA / Alteplase)
- **Indication**: Ischemic stroke within 4.5 hours of symptom onset (or last known well)
- **Dose**: Alteplase 0.9 mg/kg IV (max 90 mg); 10% as bolus over 1 minute, remainder infused over 60 minutes
- **Thai Stroke Network protocol**: Door-to-Needle target < 60 minutes (ideal < 45 minutes)

**Absolute Contraindications**:
- Intracranial hemorrhage on CT
- Active internal bleeding
- Platelet count < 100,000
- INR > 1.7 or PT > 15 seconds
- Use of direct oral anticoagulant (DOAC) within 48 hours with abnormal coagulation tests
- Blood glucose < 50 mg/dL
- SBP > 185 or DBP > 110 mmHg (after treatment attempt)
- Major surgery within 14 days

**Relative Contraindications** (individualized risk-benefit):
- NIHSS > 25 (severe stroke)
- Age > 80 years (in 3-4.5 hour window)
- Combination of prior stroke and diabetes

#### Tenecteplase (TNK) — Emerging in Thailand
- Dose: 0.25 mg/kg IV bolus (max 25 mg)
- Single bolus (easier to administer than Alteplase infusion)
- Preferred in some Thai Stroke Centers for LVO patients being transferred for thrombectomy

#### Mechanical Thrombectomy (Endovascular Treatment)
- **Indication**: Acute ischemic stroke with confirmed LVO (ICA, M1, proximal M2, basilar artery)
- **Time window**: Within 6 hours of onset (standard); up to 24 hours with favorable perfusion imaging (DAWN/DEFUSE 3 criteria)
- **Target**: Door-to-groin puncture < 90 minutes; onset-to-reperfusion < 6 hours
- Performed at Comprehensive Stroke Centers (CSC) in Thailand
- Combined IV tPA + thrombectomy (bridging therapy) if within tPA window

### Acute Phase Management (First 24-72 hours)

#### Blood Pressure Management
| Scenario | Target |
|----------|--------|
| Post IV tPA (first 24 hours) | < 180/105 mmHg |
| Candidate for thrombectomy | < 185/110 mmHg pre-procedure |
| Not receiving reperfusion therapy | Permissive hypertension: do not lower unless > 220/120 mmHg |
| After 24 hours (stable) | Gradual reduction toward < 140/90 mmHg |

- IV Labetalol 10-20 mg IV bolus; or Nicardipine infusion 5-15 mg/hr
- Avoid precipitous drops in BP

#### Antiplatelet Therapy
- **Aspirin** 160-325 mg within 24-48 hours (after hemorrhage excluded; delay 24 hours if tPA given)
- **Dual antiplatelet** (DAPT) for minor stroke (NIHSS <= 3) or high-risk TIA:
  - Aspirin 75-100 mg + Clopidogrel 75 mg daily for 21 days (CHANCE protocol), then single antiplatelet
  - Alternative: Aspirin + Ticagrelor 90 mg BID for 30 days (THALES protocol)

#### Anticoagulation (Cardioembolic Stroke)
- Start DOAC or Warfarin based on stroke severity and timing:
  - Minor stroke (NIHSS < 8): Start at day 3-5
  - Moderate stroke (NIHSS 8-15): Start at day 6-7
  - Severe stroke (NIHSS >= 16): Start at day 12-14
  - Very large infarct or hemorrhagic transformation: delay further, individualize
- Preferred DOACs: Dabigatran 150 mg BID (110 mg BID if age >= 80 or high bleed risk), Rivaroxaban 20 mg/day, Apixaban 5 mg BID (2.5 mg BID if 2 of: age >= 80, weight <= 60 kg, Cr >= 1.5), Edoxaban 60 mg/day
- Warfarin (target INR 2.0-3.0): if DOAC not available or mechanical valve

#### Statin Therapy
- High-intensity statin: Atorvastatin 40-80 mg/day or Rosuvastatin 20 mg/day
- LDL-C target: < 70 mg/dL (atherosclerotic stroke); < 100 mg/dL (others)

### Supportive Care
- Glucose management: target 140-180 mg/dL; insulin for hyperglycemia > 180 mg/dL
- Temperature management: treat fever aggressively (Paracetamol); target < 37.5 degrees C
- DVT prophylaxis: intermittent pneumatic compression (IPC) for immobilized patients; avoid prophylactic heparin in first 24 hours post-tPA
- NPO until swallowing assessment (water swallow test or videofluoroscopic study)
- Head of bed 0-30 degrees (flat in first 24 hours if large vessel occlusion and low BP)
- Decompressive craniectomy: consider in malignant MCA infarction with deterioration, age < 60

## 4. Medications

### Expected Drug Classes
- **Thrombolytics**: Alteplase 0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg), Tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg (max 25 mg)
- **Antiplatelets**: Aspirin 81-325 mg/day, Clopidogrel 75 mg/day, Ticagrelor 90 mg BID, Cilostazol 100 mg BID (especially for intracranial stenosis — more common in Asian populations)
- **Anticoagulants**: Dabigatran 110-150 mg BID, Rivaroxaban 15-20 mg/day, Apixaban 2.5-5 mg BID, Edoxaban 30-60 mg/day, Warfarin (INR 2-3)
- **Antihypertensives**: Amlodipine 5-10 mg, ACEi/ARB, IV Nicardipine, IV Labetalol
- **Statins**: Atorvastatin 40-80 mg, Rosuvastatin 10-20 mg
- **Neuroprotective agents**: Citicoline 500-2000 mg/day IV/PO (commonly used in Thai practice, though evidence limited)
- **Antiepileptics**: Levetiracetam 500-1500 mg BID, Phenytoin 300 mg/day (if post-stroke seizures)
- **Osmotic agents**: Mannitol 20% 0.25-1 g/kg IV (if cerebral edema)
- **Gastroprotection**: PPI (Omeprazole 20-40 mg) if on DAPT or anticoagulation

### Red Flag Medications
- **Alteplase beyond 4.5-hour window** (without perfusion imaging justification): increased hemorrhagic risk
- **Anticoagulation within 24 hours of tPA**: high bleeding risk; delay per protocol above
- **DOAC + antiplatelet long-term combination**: increased bleeding; generally avoid unless compelling indication (e.g., recent coronary stent)
- **Nimodipine** (only indicated for subarachnoid hemorrhage vasospasm; NOT for ischemic stroke)
- **Aggressive BP lowering in acute phase**: risk of watershed infarction extension
- **Heparin for acute ischemic stroke**: not recommended for treatment (no benefit, increased hemorrhage risk)
- **Warfarin in non-valvular AF with prior intracranial hemorrhage**: prefer DOAC or left atrial appendage closure

## 5. Follow-up and Monitoring

### Inpatient Monitoring
| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| Neurological assessment (NIHSS) | Every 1 hour x 6 hrs post-tPA, then every 2 hours x 18 hrs, then every 4 hours |
| Blood pressure | Every 15 min x 2 hrs post-tPA, then every 30 min x 6 hrs, then every hour x 16 hrs |
| Blood glucose | Every 6 hours; more frequent if insulin infusion |
| Temperature | Every 4 hours |
| Swallowing assessment | Within 24 hours of admission, before oral intake |
| Repeat CT brain | 24 hours post-tPA (or sooner if neurological deterioration) |

### Post-Discharge Follow-up
| Visit | Timing | Assessments |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| First follow-up | 1-2 weeks | BP, medication adherence, rehabilitation plan, INR if on warfarin |
| Second follow-up | 1 month | NIHSS, mRS, BP, lab review, rehabilitation progress |
| Quarterly | Every 3 months x 1 year | BP, lipids, glucose, medication review, functional status (mRS) |
| Annual | Yearly thereafter | Comprehensive vascular risk assessment, repeat imaging if indicated |

### Rehabilitation Timeline
- **Acute rehabilitation**: Begin within 24-48 hours if medically stable (early mobilization)
- **Inpatient rehabilitation**: Transfer to rehab unit when medically stable (typically day 3-7)
- **Outpatient rehabilitation**: Continue for 3-6 months minimum
- Assess: Physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, cognitive rehabilitation, psychological support
- Functional outcomes: Modified Rankin Scale (mRS) and Barthel Index at 3 months and 1 year

## 6. Length of Stay (if admitted)

### Stroke Unit (recommended for all stroke patients)
- Minor stroke (NIHSS 0-4), no complications: 3-5 days
- Moderate stroke (NIHSS 5-15): 5-10 days
- Severe stroke (NIHSS 16+) or complications: 10-21 days
- Malignant MCA infarction with craniectomy: 14-28 days
- TIA (admitted for workup): 1-3 days

### ICU Admission Criteria
- Post-tPA monitoring (first 24 hours in many Thai centers)
- NIHSS >= 16 with declining consciousness
- Post-thrombectomy monitoring
- Malignant cerebral edema
- Hemodynamic instability requiring IV antihypertensive infusion

### Discharge Criteria
- Neurologically stable for >= 24 hours (no NIHSS worsening)
- Oral medications tolerated
- Swallowing safe for oral diet (or alternative feeding established: NG tube/PEG)
- Blood pressure controlled on oral regimen
- Anticoagulation therapeutic (if cardioembolism): INR 2-3 or DOAC started
- Rehabilitation plan established
- Patient/family education completed (stroke warning signs, medication adherence, lifestyle modification)
- Follow-up appointment scheduled within 1-2 weeks

## 7. Referral Criteria

- **Comprehensive Stroke Center**: Large vessel occlusion (thrombectomy candidate), malignant MCA syndrome, posterior circulation stroke with brainstem compression, failed IV thrombolysis
- **Interventional neuroradiology**: Intracranial stenosis > 70% with recurrent events, carotid stenting (high surgical risk), intracranial aneurysm
- **Vascular surgery (Carotid endarterectomy)**: Symptomatic carotid stenosis >= 50% (NASCET criteria); optimally within 2 weeks of event
- **Cardiology/EP**: New-onset atrial fibrillation, structural heart disease, PFO with atrial septal aneurysm (closure consideration), left atrial appendage closure for anticoagulation contraindication
- **Hematology**: Hypercoagulable state, antiphospholipid syndrome, sickle cell disease
- **Rehabilitation medicine**: All patients with functional deficits (mRS >= 2); early referral within first week
- **Palliative care**: Devastating stroke with poor prognosis (bilateral large infarcts, brainstem death), family wishes for comfort care
