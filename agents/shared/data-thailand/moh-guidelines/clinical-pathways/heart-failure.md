# Thailand Clinical Pathway: Heart Failure

**Source**: Thai Heart Association (THA) / Heart Failure Council of Thailand Clinical Practice Guidelines for Heart Failure 2023
**ICD-10**: I50 (Heart Failure), I50.0 (Congestive HF), I50.1 (Left ventricular failure), I50.9 (HF unspecified), I11.0 (Hypertensive heart disease with HF), I13.0 (Hypertensive heart and renal disease with HF)
**Last Updated**: 2024

## 1. Classification / Staging

### Heart Failure Classification by LVEF (Universal Definition 2021, adopted by THA)
| Type | LVEF | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| HFrEF (Heart Failure with Reduced EF) | <= 40% | Systolic dysfunction; strongest evidence base for therapy |
| HFmrEF (Heart Failure with Mildly Reduced EF) | 41-49% | Intermediate; emerging evidence supports GDMT |
| HFpEF (Heart Failure with Preserved EF) | >= 50% | Diastolic dysfunction; limited evidence for most therapies |
| HFimpEF (Heart Failure with Improved EF) | Previously <= 40%, now > 40% | Continue GDMT; do not discontinue |

### AHA/ACC Stages (used in Thai guidelines)
| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| A | At risk for HF (hypertension, diabetes, CAD, cardiotoxin exposure) without structural disease or symptoms |
| B | Structural heart disease (LVH, reduced EF, valvular disease) without HF symptoms |
| C | Structural heart disease with current or prior HF symptoms |
| D | Advanced/refractory HF requiring specialized interventions |

### NYHA Functional Classification
| Class | Symptoms |
|-------|----------|
| I | No limitation of physical activity |
| II | Slight limitation; comfortable at rest; ordinary activity causes symptoms |
| III | Marked limitation; comfortable at rest; less than ordinary activity causes symptoms |
| IV | Unable to carry on any physical activity without discomfort; symptoms at rest |

### Acute Heart Failure Profiles (Stevenson Classification)
| Profile | Congestion | Perfusion | Description |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Warm and Dry | No | Adequate | Compensated |
| Warm and Wet | Yes | Adequate | Most common acute presentation; diuretics |
| Cold and Wet | Yes | Low | Cardiogenic shock; inotropes + diuretics |
| Cold and Dry | No | Low | Volume depletion; cautious fluid challenge |

## 2. Initial Workup

### Standard Investigations
- **NT-proBNP or BNP** (MANDATORY for diagnosis): NT-proBNP > 125 pg/mL (chronic) or > 300 pg/mL (acute) suggestive; BNP > 35 pg/mL (chronic) or > 100 pg/mL (acute)
- Transthoracic echocardiography (TTE): LVEF, wall motion, valve function, diastolic function, RV assessment, pericardial effusion
- 12-lead ECG: LVH, atrial fibrillation, bundle branch block, ischemic changes, QTc
- Chest X-ray: cardiomegaly (CTR > 0.5), pulmonary congestion, pleural effusion
- CBC: anemia assessment (common comorbidity in Thai HF population)
- Serum electrolytes (Na, K, Mg): hyponatremia is prognostic marker
- Rerum creatinine with eGFR (CKD-EPI)
- Liver function tests (AST, ALT, bilirubin, albumin): hepatic congestion
- Fasting glucose and HbA1c
- Fasting lipid profile
- Thyroid function tests (TSH, FT4): thyroid disease is reversible cause
- Iron studies (serum ferritin, TSAT): iron deficiency common (ferritin < 100, or 100-299 with TSAT < 20%)
- Urinalysis and UACR

### Conditional Investigations
- Coronary angiography: if ischemic etiology suspected, angina symptoms, or high-risk features
- Cardiac MRI (CMR): myocarditis, infiltrative disease (amyloidosis), iron overload, viability assessment
- Right heart catheterization: pre-transplant evaluation, pulmonary hypertension assessment, difficult fluid management
- Stress echocardiography or nuclear perfusion: ischemia assessment if non-invasive approach preferred
- Holter monitoring / event recorder: if arrhythmia suspected (palpitations, syncope, wide QRS)
- Cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET): functional capacity, transplant candidacy (peak VO2)
- Endomyocardial biopsy: suspected infiltrative/inflammatory disease not diagnosed by CMR
- Genetic testing: familial dilated cardiomyopathy, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, arrhythmogenic cardiomyopathy

## 3. Treatment Protocol

### Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT) for HFrEF (the "4 Pillars")

The THA 2023 guidelines recommend simultaneous initiation of all four pillars at low doses with rapid uptitration (within 1-2 weeks per step) rather than sequential addition.

#### Pillar 1: RAAS Inhibitor (ACEi/ARB or ARNI)
- **ARNI (preferred)**: Sacubitril/Valsartan — start 24/26 mg BID if naive or SBP borderline, target 97/103 mg BID
  - Requires 36-hour washout from ACEi before switching
- **ACEi**: Enalapril 2.5 mg BID, target 10-20 mg BID; or Ramipril 1.25 mg/day, target 10 mg/day
- **ARB** (if ACEi intolerant due to cough): Valsartan 40 mg BID, target 160 mg BID; or Losartan 25 mg/day, target 150 mg/day; or Candesartan 4 mg/day, target 32 mg/day

#### Pillar 2: Beta-blocker
- **Bisoprolol**: Start 1.25 mg/day, target 10 mg/day
- **Carvedilol**: Start 3.125 mg BID, target 25 mg BID (50 mg BID if > 85 kg)
- **Metoprolol succinate (CR/XL)**: Start 12.5-25 mg/day, target 200 mg/day
- Titrate every 2 weeks; do NOT initiate during acute decompensation

#### Pillar 3: Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonist (MRA)
- **Spironolactone**: 12.5-25 mg/day, target 25-50 mg/day
- **Eplerenone**: 25 mg/day, target 50 mg/day (fewer gynecomastia side effects)
- Requirement: K < 5.0 mEq/L and eGFR > 30 mL/min; monitor K and Cr within 1 week of initiation

#### Pillar 4: SGLT2 Inhibitor
- **Dapagliflozin** 10 mg/day — proven benefit regardless of diabetes status (DAPA-HF)
- **Empagliflozin** 10 mg/day — proven benefit regardless of diabetes status (EMPEROR-Reduced)
- Can be initiated early, even during hospitalization for acute HF
- Continue if LVEF improves above 40% (evidence in HFmrEF and HFpEF as well)

### Diuretics (Symptom Relief — not a "pillar" but essential)
- **Furosemide**: 20-80 mg/day PO (up to 400-600 mg/day IV in acute decompensation)
- **Bumetanide**: 0.5-2 mg/day PO (alternative to furosemide)
- **Torasemide**: 10-20 mg/day PO (better bioavailability than furosemide)
- **Metolazone** 2.5-5 mg PO: add for diuretic resistance (sequential nephron blockade)
- Target: euvolemia with lowest effective diuretic dose; titrate based on daily weight and symptoms
- IV diuretics for acute decompensation: Furosemide 40-80 mg IV bolus, or continuous infusion 5-40 mg/hr

### Additional Therapies
- **Hydralazine/Isosorbide dinitrate**: Hydralazine 25-75 mg TID + ISDN 20-40 mg TID — if ACEi/ARB/ARNI intolerant (renal failure or hyperkalemia)
- **Ivabradine**: 5-7.5 mg BID — if sinus rhythm, HR >= 70 bpm despite maximally tolerated beta-blocker
- **Digoxin**: 0.125-0.25 mg/day — for symptom control in AF with rapid ventricular rate, or persistent symptoms despite GDMT; target level 0.5-0.9 ng/mL
- **IV iron (Ferric carboxymaltose)**: If ferritin < 100 ng/mL, or ferritin 100-299 with TSAT < 20%; improves symptoms and exercise capacity
- **Vericiguat**: 2.5 mg/day titrated to 10 mg/day — for worsening HF despite GDMT (limited availability in Thailand)

### Device Therapy
- **ICD (Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator)**: LVEF <= 35% despite >= 3 months optimal GDMT, NYHA II-III, life expectancy > 1 year
- **CRT (Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy)**: LVEF <= 35%, LBBB with QRS >= 150 ms, NYHA II-IV despite GDMT; CRT-D preferred
- **CRT consideration**: LBBB with QRS 130-149 ms, or non-LBBB with QRS >= 150 ms (weaker indication)

### HFpEF Treatment (THA 2023)
- SGLT2 inhibitor: Empagliflozin or Dapagliflozin (EMPEROR-Preserved, DELIVER trials)
- Diuretics for congestion
- Treat comorbidities aggressively: hypertension, AF, obesity, diabetes, CAD
- GLP-1 RA for obese HFpEF patients with diabetes (Semaglutide — STEP-HFpEF)
- MRA may benefit (TOPCAT trial, post-hoc analysis)

### Treatment Targets
| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Heart rate | 60-70 bpm (sinus rhythm) |
| Blood pressure | SBP 100-130 mmHg (tolerated) |
| Body weight | Dry weight; daily monitoring |
| Serum Na | > 130 mEq/L |
| Serum K | 4.0-5.0 mEq/L |
| eGFR | Stable; up to 30% decline acceptable after RAAS/SGLT2i initiation |
| NT-proBNP | Trending downward (no absolute target) |
| NYHA class | Improvement by at least 1 class |
| Iron status | Ferritin > 100 ng/mL, TSAT > 20% |

## 4. Medications

### Expected Drug Classes
- **ARNI**: Sacubitril/Valsartan 24/26 mg to 97/103 mg BID
- **ACE inhibitors**: Enalapril 2.5-20 mg BID, Ramipril 1.25-10 mg/day, Lisinopril 2.5-40 mg/day, Perindopril 2-8 mg/day
- **ARBs**: Valsartan 40-160 mg BID, Candesartan 4-32 mg/day, Losartan 25-150 mg/day
- **Beta-blockers**: Bisoprolol 1.25-10 mg/day, Carvedilol 3.125-25 mg BID, Metoprolol succinate 12.5-200 mg/day
- **MRAs**: Spironolactone 12.5-50 mg/day, Eplerenone 25-50 mg/day
- **SGLT2 inhibitors**: Dapagliflozin 10 mg/day, Empagliflozin 10 mg/day
- **Loop diuretics**: Furosemide 20-600 mg/day, Bumetanide 0.5-10 mg/day, Torasemide 10-200 mg/day
- **Thiazide-like diuretics**: Metolazone 2.5-10 mg/day (add-on for diuretic resistance)
- **Vasodilators**: Hydralazine 25-75 mg TID, Isosorbide dinitrate 20-40 mg TID
- **Sinus node inhibitor**: Ivabradine 5-7.5 mg BID
- **Cardiac glycoside**: Digoxin 0.0625-0.25 mg/day (target level 0.5-0.9 ng/mL)
- **Inotropes** (acute use only): Dobutamine 2-20 mcg/kg/min, Milrinone 0.375-0.75 mcg/kg/min, Levosimendan 0.05-0.2 mcg/kg/min
- **IV Iron**: Ferric carboxymaltose (Ferinject) 500-1000 mg IV per session
- **Anticoagulants** (if AF): Warfarin, DOACs as per AF guidelines

### Red Flag Medications
- **NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Naproxen, COX-2 inhibitors)**: CONTRAINDICATED — sodium/fluid retention, worsen HF, reduce diuretic and RAAS inhibitor efficacy
- **Non-DHP CCBs (Verapamil, Diltiazem)**: CONTRAINDICATED in HFrEF — negative inotropic effect
- **DHP CCBs (Amlodipine, Felodipine)**: Use with caution; generally safe but do not improve outcomes in HFrEF
- **Thiazolidinediones (Pioglitazone, Rosiglitazone)**: CONTRAINDICATED — fluid retention, worsen HF
- **Class I antiarrhythmics (Flecainide, Propafenone)**: CONTRAINDICATED in HFrEF — proarrhythmic
- **Dronedarone**: CONTRAINDICATED in NYHA III-IV or recent decompensation
- **Metformin**: SAFE in stable HF (previously contraindicated; now recommended if eGFR > 30)
- **Triple RAAS blockade (ACEi + ARB + MRA)**: NEVER combine ACEi + ARB; dual RAAS blockade contraindicated
- **High-dose digoxin (level > 1.2 ng/mL)**: Increased mortality; maintain level 0.5-0.9 ng/mL
- **Alpha-blockers (Doxazosin)**: May worsen HF (ALLHAT trial); avoid as antihypertensive in HF

## 5. Follow-up and Monitoring

### Inpatient Monitoring (Acute Decompensation)
| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| Vital signs (HR, BP, SpO2) | Every 4-6 hours; continuous if ICU |
| Daily weight | Every morning (same scale, same conditions) |
| Intake and output | Strict I&O every shift |
| Serum electrolytes (Na, K) | Daily during active diuresis |
| Serum creatinine / eGFR | Daily during active diuresis; every 2-3 days when stable |
| NT-proBNP / BNP | On admission and at discharge (trending) |
| Digoxin level | If on digoxin: at steady state (5-7 days), then if renal function changes |

### Post-Discharge Monitoring (Critical Vulnerable Period: first 30 days)
| Visit | Timing | Assessments |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| Early follow-up | Within 7 days of discharge | Weight, BP, HR, symptoms, medication review, electrolytes + Cr |
| 2-week follow-up | 2 weeks | Uptitrate GDMT, reassess volume status, lab check |
| 1-month follow-up | 4 weeks | GDMT optimization, NYHA assessment, NT-proBNP |
| 3-month follow-up | 12 weeks | Echo if not recent, iron studies, GDMT at target doses? |
| Ongoing | Every 3-6 months | Clinical assessment, labs, GDMT adherence, device check if applicable |
| Annual | Yearly | Comprehensive: Echo, full labs, functional assessment, device interrogation |

### Laboratory Monitoring
| Test | Timing |
|------|--------|
| Na, K, Cr, eGFR | 1-2 weeks after RAAS/ARNI/MRA initiation or dose change; then every 3-6 months |
| NT-proBNP | At diagnosis, during hospitalization, at stable state; trending over time |
| HbA1c | Every 6 months if diabetic |
| Iron studies | Every 6-12 months; repeat after IV iron |
| Liver function | Every 6-12 months |
| Digoxin level | After initiation (5-7 days), then with any renal change or suspected toxicity |
| TSH | Annually if on amiodarone; otherwise as indicated |

### Patient Self-Monitoring
- Daily weight: same time, same scale; report gain > 2 kg in 3 days
- Fluid restriction: 1.5-2 L/day (individualized)
- Sodium restriction: < 2 g/day
- Symptom diary: dyspnea, orthopnea, edema, exercise tolerance
- Medication adherence tracking

## 6. Length of Stay (if admitted)

### Expected LOS for Acute Decompensated Heart Failure (ADHF)
- Uncomplicated ADHF (warm and wet, responds to diuretics): 4-7 days
- ADHF with renal impairment or diuretic resistance: 7-14 days
- Cardiogenic shock requiring inotropes/vasopressors: 10-21 days
- New-onset HF requiring full workup and GDMT initiation: 5-10 days
- Advanced HF evaluation (transplant/LVAD workup): 14-28 days

### ICU Admission Criteria
- Cardiogenic shock (cold and wet): SBP < 90, organ hypoperfusion
- Requiring IV inotropes or vasopressors
- Acute pulmonary edema with respiratory failure (NIV or intubation)
- Complex arrhythmia (VT/VF storm)
- Acute mechanical complication (acute MR, VSD)
- Post-cardiac arrest

### Discharge Criteria (THA 2023)
- Symptoms improved: NYHA improved by >= 1 class from admission
- Oral diuretic regimen established; on stable oral medications for >= 24 hours
- Weight stable (not gaining) for >= 24 hours
- Resting HR < 100 bpm; SBP >= 90 mmHg
- Serum Na > 130 mEq/L; K 3.5-5.0 mEq/L
- Cr stable or improving
- NT-proBNP decreased by >= 30% from admission (or absolute decline)
- All 4 GDMT pillars initiated (even at low doses) before discharge
- IV diuretics discontinued for >= 24 hours; transition to oral complete
- Iron deficiency addressed (IV iron given before discharge if indicated)
- Patient education completed: daily weight, fluid restriction, medication adherence, warning signs, action plan
- Follow-up appointment within 7 days
- Referral to cardiac rehabilitation if appropriate

## 7. Referral Criteria

- **Advanced HF specialist / Transplant center**: NYHA III-IV despite optimal GDMT, recurrent hospitalizations (>= 2/year), escalating diuretic requirements, progressive renal dysfunction, need for inotropic support, consideration for LVAD or heart transplant
- **Electrophysiologist**: ICD/CRT candidacy (LVEF <= 35%, QRS >= 130 ms), recurrent VT/VF, AF requiring rhythm control or ablation, pacemaker evaluation
- **Interventional cardiologist**: Ischemic evaluation (coronary angiography), percutaneous valve intervention (TEER for MR)
- **Cardiac surgeon**: Valve surgery (severe MR/AS contributing to HF), CABG if viable myocardium, LVAD implantation
- **Nephrologist**: Cardiorenal syndrome (worsening renal function despite decongestion), eGFR < 30, need for ultrafiltration
- **Palliative care**: Stage D refractory HF not candidate for transplant/LVAD, recurrent hospitalizations with declining trajectory, symptom management focus
- **Cardiac rehabilitation**: All stable HF patients (NYHA I-III); exercise-based rehabilitation improves functional capacity and reduces hospitalization
- **Endocrinologist**: Cardiac amyloidosis (ATTR or AL), thyroid cardiomyopathy, uncontrolled diabetes with HF
