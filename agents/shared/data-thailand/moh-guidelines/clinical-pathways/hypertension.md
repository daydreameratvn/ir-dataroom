# Thailand Clinical Pathway: Essential Hypertension

**Source**: Thai Hypertension Society (THS) Guidelines for the Management of Hypertension 2019 (updated 2023)
**ICD-10**: I10 (Essential/Primary Hypertension), I11-I13 (Hypertensive heart/renal disease)
**Last Updated**: 2024

## 1. Classification / Staging

### Blood Pressure Classification (THS 2019/2023)
| Category | SBP (mmHg) | | DBP (mmHg) |
|----------|------------|---|------------|
| Optimal | < 120 | and | < 80 |
| Normal | 120-129 | and/or | 80-84 |
| High Normal | 130-139 | and/or | 85-89 |
| Grade 1 Hypertension | 140-159 | and/or | 90-99 |
| Grade 2 Hypertension | 160-179 | and/or | 100-109 |
| Grade 3 Hypertension | >= 180 | and/or | >= 110 |
| Isolated Systolic HTN | >= 140 | and | < 90 |

### Cardiovascular Risk Stratification
| Risk Factors | High Normal | Grade 1 | Grade 2 | Grade 3 |
|-------------|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| No other RF | Low | Low | Moderate | High |
| 1-2 RF | Low | Moderate | Moderate-High | High |
| >= 3 RF | Low-Moderate | Moderate-High | High | High |
| OD, CKD stage 3, or DM | Moderate-High | High | High | Very High |
| Established CVD, CKD 4-5 | Very High | Very High | Very High | Very High |

RF = Risk Factors; OD = Organ Damage; DM = Diabetes Mellitus; CVD = Cardiovascular Disease

### Target Organ Damage Assessment
- Left ventricular hypertrophy (ECG or echocardiography)
- Carotid intima-media thickness > 0.9 mm or plaque
- Pulse wave velocity (carotid-femoral) > 10 m/s
- Ankle-brachial index < 0.9
- eGFR 30-60 mL/min/1.73m2 or microalbuminuria (UACR 30-300 mg/g)
- Retinopathy grade III-IV

## 2. Initial Workup

### Standard Investigations
- Office blood pressure: at least 2 readings, 1-2 minutes apart, on 2 separate occasions
- Ambulatory BP monitoring (ABPM) or Home BP monitoring (HBPM) to confirm diagnosis
- CBC
- Fasting plasma glucose and HbA1c
- Fasting lipid profile (Total cholesterol, LDL-C, HDL-C, Triglycerides)
- Serum creatinine with eGFR (CKD-EPI)
- Serum electrolytes (Na, K)
- Serum uric acid
- Urinalysis and UACR (urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio)
- 12-lead ECG
- Fundoscopic examination

### Conditional Investigations
- Echocardiography: if ECG suggests LVH, or Grade 2-3 hypertension, or suspected heart failure
- Carotid ultrasound: if bruit present or high cardiovascular risk
- Renal artery duplex/CT angiography: if suspected renovascular hypertension (refractory HTN, renal bruit, asymmetric kidneys, young onset)
- Plasma aldosterone/renin ratio: if suspected primary aldosteronism (hypokalemia, resistant HTN, adrenal incidentaloma)
- 24-hour urine catecholamines/metanephrines: if suspected pheochromocytoma (paroxysmal HTN, headache, diaphoresis, palpitations)
- Thyroid function tests (TSH, FT4): if clinical suspicion of thyroid disease
- Sleep study (polysomnography): if suspected obstructive sleep apnea (obesity, snoring, daytime somnolence)
- CT/MRI adrenals: if biochemical evidence of secondary hypertension

## 3. Treatment Protocol

### Lifestyle Modification (all patients, 3-6 months trial for Grade 1 with low-moderate risk)
- Sodium restriction: < 5 g/day (< 2 g sodium), reduce fish sauce and salty condiments (common in Thai diet)
- DASH-style diet adapted for Thai cuisine: increase vegetables, fruits, low-fat dairy; reduce saturated fat
- Weight management: target BMI < 23 kg/m2 (Asian criteria); waist circumference < 90 cm (male), < 80 cm (female)
- Physical activity: at least 150 min/week moderate-intensity aerobic exercise (e.g., brisk walking, swimming)
- Alcohol limitation: <= 2 standard drinks/day (men), <= 1 standard drink/day (women)
- Smoking cessation
- Stress management

### First-line Treatment (monotherapy or initial combination)

**Monotherapy** (Grade 1 HTN with low-moderate risk):
- ACE inhibitor: Enalapril 5-20 mg/day or Ramipril 2.5-10 mg/day
- ARB: Losartan 50-100 mg/day or Valsartan 80-160 mg/day or Olmesartan 20-40 mg/day or Telmisartan 40-80 mg/day
- Calcium channel blocker (CCB): Amlodipine 5-10 mg/day or Felodipine 5-10 mg/day or Nifedipine GITS 30-60 mg/day
- Thiazide/thiazide-like diuretic: Hydrochlorothiazide 12.5-25 mg/day or Indapamide 1.5 mg/day or Chlorthalidone 12.5-25 mg/day

**Initial Dual Combination** (Grade 2-3 HTN, or Grade 1 with high/very high risk):
- ACEi/ARB + CCB (preferred): e.g., Amlodipine/Valsartan, Amlodipine/Ramipril
- ACEi/ARB + Diuretic: e.g., Losartan/HCTZ, Enalapril/HCTZ
- CCB + Diuretic (if RAAS inhibitor contraindicated)

### Second-line Treatment (if target not reached on dual therapy after 4-8 weeks)
**Triple Combination**:
- ACEi/ARB + CCB + Thiazide diuretic (preferred triple)

### Resistant Hypertension (uncontrolled on optimal triple therapy)
- Add Spironolactone 25-50 mg/day (preferred 4th agent per THS)
- Alternative: Alpha-blocker (Doxazosin 2-8 mg/day) or Beta-blocker (Bisoprolol 5-10 mg/day) or Centrally acting (Methyldopa 250-500 mg BID)
- Consider referral to hypertension specialist
- Rule out secondary causes and non-adherence

### Specific Indications for Drug Selection (THS)
| Compelling Indication | Preferred Agent(s) |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| Diabetes mellitus | ACEi or ARB (renoprotective) |
| CKD with proteinuria | ACEi or ARB |
| Coronary artery disease | ACEi/ARB + Beta-blocker |
| Heart failure (HFrEF) | ACEi/ARB (or ARNI) + Beta-blocker + MRA + SGLT2i |
| Stroke prevention | ACEi/ARB + CCB or Diuretic |
| Atrial fibrillation (rate control) | Beta-blocker or Non-DHP CCB (Diltiazem/Verapamil) |
| Pregnancy | Methyldopa, Labetalol, or Nifedipine |
| Elderly (>= 80 years) | Start low, go slow; CCB or Diuretic preferred |

### Treatment Targets (THS 2023)
| Population | Office BP Target |
|-----------|-----------------|
| General (age < 65) | < 130/80 mmHg (if tolerated) |
| Age 65-79 | 130-139 / < 80 mmHg |
| Age >= 80 | 130-139 / < 80 mmHg (if tolerated); < 150/90 if frail |
| Diabetes | < 130/80 mmHg |
| CKD | < 130/80 mmHg (with proteinuria); < 140/90 (without proteinuria) |
| Coronary artery disease | < 130/80 mmHg |
| Post-stroke (chronic) | < 130/80 mmHg |
| Home BP equivalent | < 125/75 mmHg |

## 4. Medications

### Expected Drug Classes
- **ACE inhibitors**: Enalapril 5-40 mg/day, Ramipril 2.5-10 mg/day, Lisinopril 10-40 mg/day, Perindopril 4-8 mg/day
- **ARBs**: Losartan 50-100 mg/day, Valsartan 80-320 mg/day, Olmesartan 20-40 mg/day, Telmisartan 40-80 mg/day, Candesartan 8-32 mg/day, Irbesartan 150-300 mg/day
- **CCBs (Dihydropyridine)**: Amlodipine 5-10 mg/day, Felodipine 5-10 mg/day, Nifedipine GITS 30-60 mg/day, Manidipine 10-20 mg/day
- **CCBs (Non-Dihydropyridine)**: Diltiazem 120-360 mg/day, Verapamil 120-480 mg/day
- **Thiazide/Thiazide-like diuretics**: Hydrochlorothiazide 12.5-25 mg/day, Indapamide 1.5 mg/day, Chlorthalidone 12.5-25 mg/day
- **Beta-blockers**: Bisoprolol 2.5-10 mg/day, Carvedilol 12.5-50 mg/day, Atenolol 25-100 mg/day, Metoprolol succinate 25-200 mg/day, Nebivolol 5-10 mg/day
- **Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists**: Spironolactone 25-50 mg/day, Eplerenone 25-50 mg/day
- **Alpha-blockers**: Doxazosin 1-8 mg/day, Prazosin 1-10 mg/day
- **Centrally acting**: Methyldopa 250-1000 mg/day (primarily for pregnancy)
- **Direct vasodilators**: Hydralazine 25-100 mg BID (adjunctive use)

### Red Flag Medications
- **ACEi + ARB combination**: Contraindicated (hyperkalemia, renal impairment risk; ONTARGET study)
- **ACEi/ARB in pregnancy**: Contraindicated (teratogenic); use Methyldopa, Labetalol, or Nifedipine
- **Non-selective beta-blockers in asthma/COPD**: Avoid Propranolol; use cardioselective (Bisoprolol)
- **Verapamil/Diltiazem + Beta-blocker**: Risk of severe bradycardia and AV block
- **Thiazide diuretics in severe gout**: May precipitate acute gout flare; use with caution
- **Spironolactone with K > 5.0 mEq/L or eGFR < 30**: Risk of life-threatening hyperkalemia
- **Atenolol as sole agent**: Inferior stroke prevention; not preferred first-line per THS
- **Short-acting Nifedipine (sublingual)**: Avoid for hypertensive emergencies; risk of precipitous BP drop

## 5. Follow-up and Monitoring

### Monitoring Schedule
| Phase | Frequency | Assessments |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Initial titration (first 3 months) | Every 2-4 weeks | Office BP, symptoms, medication side effects, K/Cr if on RAAS inhibitors |
| Dose stabilization | Monthly until target BP achieved | Office BP, adherence assessment |
| Stable on treatment | Every 3-6 months | Office BP, HBPM review, medication adherence |
| Annual comprehensive | Yearly | Full laboratory panel, ECG, fundoscopy, UACR, cardiovascular risk reassessment |

### Laboratory Monitoring
| Test | Timing |
|------|--------|
| Serum K, Creatinine, eGFR | 1-2 weeks after starting/changing ACEi/ARB/diuretic/MRA; then every 6-12 months |
| Fasting glucose, HbA1c | Annually (thiazide/beta-blocker metabolic effects) |
| Lipid profile | Annually |
| Uric acid | Annually (especially if on thiazide diuretics) |
| UACR | Annually |
| Serum Na | After starting/changing diuretic; monitor elderly for hyponatremia |

### Home Blood Pressure Monitoring (HBPM)
- Recommended for all hypertensive patients
- Validated oscillometric upper-arm device
- Measure twice in the morning (before medication, before breakfast) and twice in the evening, for 7 consecutive days
- Discard first day; average remaining readings
- HBPM target: < 135/85 mmHg (general); < 125/75 mmHg (high risk)

## 6. Length of Stay (if admitted)

### Hypertensive Emergency (SBP >= 180 and/or DBP >= 120 with acute organ damage)
- ICU/CCU admission: 2-5 days
- IV antihypertensives: Nicardipine infusion 5-15 mg/hr, Labetalol IV, or Nitroprusside (aortic dissection)
- Target: Reduce MAP by no more than 25% in first hour, then gradually to 160/100 over 2-6 hours

### Hypertensive Urgency (SBP >= 180 and/or DBP >= 120 without acute organ damage)
- Observation: 4-6 hours in emergency department
- Oral medications: Captopril 25 mg SL/PO, Amlodipine 5-10 mg PO, or adjust existing regimen
- Outpatient follow-up within 24-72 hours

### Discharge Criteria
- BP < 160/100 mmHg on oral medications
- No evidence of acute organ damage (normal troponin, creatinine stable, no neurological deficits)
- Oral medication regimen established and tolerated
- Patient educated on adherence, HBPM, warning signs
- Follow-up appointment within 1-2 weeks

## 7. Referral Criteria

- **Hypertension specialist/internist**: Resistant hypertension (uncontrolled on optimal triple therapy including diuretic), suspected secondary hypertension, hypertension in pregnancy
- **Nephrologist**: eGFR < 30 mL/min, rapidly declining renal function, refractory hyperkalemia on RAAS blockade
- **Cardiologist**: Hypertensive heart disease with HF symptoms, LVH with arrhythmia, acute coronary syndrome
- **Endocrinologist**: Primary aldosteronism confirmed, pheochromocytoma, Cushing syndrome
- **Neurologist**: Hypertensive encephalopathy, stroke or TIA
- **Ophthalmologist**: Hypertensive retinopathy grade III-IV, papilledema
- **Vascular surgeon**: Renovascular hypertension amenable to intervention
