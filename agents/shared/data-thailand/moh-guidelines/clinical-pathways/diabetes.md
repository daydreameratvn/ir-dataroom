# Thailand Clinical Pathway: Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

**Source**: Thai Diabetes Association (TDA) Clinical Practice Guidelines for Diabetes 2023
**ICD-10**: E11 (Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus), E11.0-E11.9 (with complications), E10 (Type 1 for reference)
**Last Updated**: 2024

## 1. Classification / Staging

### Diagnostic Criteria (any one of the following)
- Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) >= 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L) on two occasions
- 2-hour Plasma Glucose >= 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) during 75g OGTT
- HbA1c >= 6.5% (48 mmol/mol) — confirmed by NGSP-certified method
- Random Plasma Glucose >= 200 mg/dL with classic symptoms (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss)

### Prediabetes
- Impaired Fasting Glucose (IFG): FPG 100-125 mg/dL
- Impaired Glucose Tolerance (IGT): 2-hr PG 140-199 mg/dL
- HbA1c 5.7-6.4%

### Risk Stratification at Diagnosis
| Risk Level | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| Low risk | HbA1c < 7.5%, no complications, age < 60 |
| Moderate risk | HbA1c 7.5-9.0%, or 1-2 microvascular complications |
| High risk | HbA1c > 9.0%, or macrovascular disease, or CKD stage 3+ |
| Very high risk | HbA1c > 9.0% with symptoms, DKA/HHS, or multiple organ involvement |

## 2. Initial Workup

### Standard Investigations
- Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG)
- HbA1c
- Fasting lipid profile (Total cholesterol, LDL-C, HDL-C, Triglycerides)
- Serum creatinine with eGFR calculation (CKD-EPI)
- Urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR)
- Liver function tests (AST, ALT) — baseline before metformin/statin
- CBC
- Electrolytes (Na, K)
- Thyroid function (TSH) if clinically indicated
- ECG (12-lead)
- Fundoscopic examination or retinal photography
- Foot examination (monofilament test, pedal pulses, ABI if indicated)

### Conditional Investigations
- C-peptide and anti-GAD antibodies: if age < 30 or suspected LADA/Type 1
- Fructosamine: if HbA1c unreliable (hemoglobinopathy, chronic anemia, pregnancy)
- Cardiac stress test: if multiple cardiovascular risk factors or symptoms
- Carotid duplex ultrasound: if known atherosclerotic disease or age > 50 with risk factors
- Nerve conduction study: if clinical neuropathy symptoms present
- Ankle-brachial index (ABI): if peripheral vascular disease suspected

## 3. Treatment Protocol

### Lifestyle Modification (all patients)
- Medical nutrition therapy (MNT): carbohydrate counting, 45-60% calories from carbohydrate
- Thai-specific dietary counseling: reduce white rice portions, substitute with brown/riceberry rice
- Physical activity: at least 150 min/week moderate-intensity aerobic exercise
- Weight reduction target: 5-10% body weight if BMI >= 23 kg/m2 (Asian criteria)
- Smoking cessation counseling

### First-line Pharmacotherapy
- **Metformin** 500 mg once daily, titrate over 2-4 weeks to 1000-2000 mg/day in divided doses
  - Start with extended-release formulation if GI intolerance
  - Contraindicated if eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m2; reduce dose if eGFR 30-45

### Second-line Treatment (add to metformin if HbA1c not at target after 3 months)
Based on TDA 2023 patient-centered approach:

| Clinical Priority | Preferred Add-on |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Established ASCVD or high CV risk | SGLT2 inhibitor (empagliflozin 10-25 mg or dapagliflozin 10 mg) or GLP-1 RA (liraglutide 1.2-1.8 mg or semaglutide 0.5-1.0 mg) |
| Heart failure (HFrEF or HFpEF) | SGLT2 inhibitor (empagliflozin 10 mg or dapagliflozin 10 mg) |
| CKD (albuminuria or eGFR 25-60) | SGLT2 inhibitor (preferred) or GLP-1 RA |
| Need to minimize hypoglycemia | DPP-4 inhibitor, SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA, or TZD |
| Need to promote weight loss | GLP-1 RA (preferred) or SGLT2 inhibitor |
| Cost is primary concern | Sulfonylurea (glipizide 5-20 mg or gliclazide MR 30-120 mg) or TZD (pioglitazone 15-30 mg) |

### Third-line / Intensification
- Triple oral combination therapy, or
- Add basal insulin (NPH or glargine or detemir) if HbA1c remains above target
- Basal insulin starting dose: 10 units or 0.1-0.2 units/kg/day at bedtime
- Titrate by 2-4 units every 3-7 days targeting FPG 80-130 mg/dL

### Insulin Therapy (when indicated)
- **Basal insulin**: Glargine (Lantus/biosimilar) 10 U at bedtime, or NPH 10 U at bedtime
- **Basal-bolus**: Add rapid-acting insulin (lispro, aspart, or glulisine) before largest meal, then expand
- **Premixed insulin**: 70/30 (NPH/regular) or analogue premix (e.g., BiAsp 30) — twice daily
- HbA1c > 10% or symptomatic hyperglycemia at diagnosis: start insulin immediately

### Treatment Targets (TDA 2023)
| Parameter | General Target | Elderly/Frail (> 70 or limited life expectancy) |
|-----------|---------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| HbA1c | < 7.0% (53 mmol/mol) | < 8.0% (64 mmol/mol) |
| FPG | 80-130 mg/dL | 100-180 mg/dL |
| 2-hr PPG | < 180 mg/dL | < 200 mg/dL |
| Blood pressure | < 130/80 mmHg | < 140/80 mmHg |
| LDL-C | < 100 mg/dL (< 70 if ASCVD) | < 100 mg/dL |
| Triglycerides | < 150 mg/dL | < 150 mg/dL |

## 4. Medications

### Expected Drug Classes
- **Biguanides**: Metformin 500-2000 mg/day (first-line)
- **Sulfonylureas**: Gliclazide MR 30-120 mg/day, Glipizide 5-20 mg/day, Glimepiride 1-4 mg/day
- **DPP-4 inhibitors**: Sitagliptin 100 mg/day, Vildagliptin 50 mg BID, Linagliptin 5 mg/day, Saxagliptin 5 mg/day
- **SGLT2 inhibitors**: Empagliflozin 10-25 mg/day, Dapagliflozin 10 mg/day, Canagliflozin 100-300 mg/day
- **GLP-1 receptor agonists**: Liraglutide 0.6-1.8 mg/day SC, Semaglutide 0.25-1.0 mg/week SC, Dulaglutide 0.75-1.5 mg/week SC
- **Thiazolidinediones**: Pioglitazone 15-45 mg/day
- **Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors**: Acarbose 25-100 mg TID, Voglibose 0.2-0.3 mg TID
- **Insulins**: NPH, Glargine, Detemir, Degludec (basal); Lispro, Aspart, Glulisine (rapid); Premixed
- **Statins**: Atorvastatin 10-80 mg, Rosuvastatin 5-20 mg, Simvastatin 10-40 mg (for dyslipidemia management)
- **Antihypertensives**: ACE inhibitor or ARB preferred (renoprotective)

### Red Flag Medications
- **Glibenclamide (Glyburide)**: Higher hypoglycemia risk, especially in elderly and CKD; avoid if eGFR < 60
- **Rosiglitazone**: Withdrawn in Thailand due to cardiovascular risk
- **Metformin with eGFR < 30**: Contraindicated; lactic acidosis risk
- **Pioglitazone in heart failure**: Contraindicated (NYHA Class III-IV); fluid retention
- **SGLT2 inhibitors in recurrent UTI/genital infections**: Use with caution; requires patient education
- **Sulfonylureas in hepatic/renal impairment**: Increased hypoglycemia risk; prefer gliclazide if SU needed

## 5. Follow-up and Monitoring

### Routine Monitoring Schedule
| Test | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| HbA1c | Every 3 months until at target, then every 6 months |
| FPG / Self-monitoring blood glucose (SMBG) | Per visit; daily if on insulin |
| Fasting lipid profile | Annually; repeat 6-12 weeks after statin initiation/change |
| Serum creatinine + eGFR | Annually; every 3-6 months if CKD present |
| UACR (urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio) | Annually |
| Liver function (ALT) | Annually |
| Fundoscopic exam / retinal photography | Annually (or per ophthalmologist if retinopathy present) |
| Comprehensive foot exam | Annually; more frequent if neuropathy/PAD |
| Blood pressure | Every visit |
| Body weight / BMI | Every visit |
| ECG | Annually if > 40 years or cardiovascular risk factors |

### Self-Monitoring Blood Glucose (SMBG)
- On oral agents only: FPG and post-meal 2-3 times/week
- On basal insulin: FPG daily
- On basal-bolus insulin: FPG + pre-meal + 2-hr post-meal; at least 4 times/day
- CGM (continuous glucose monitoring) if available and indicated

## 6. Length of Stay (if admitted)

### Admission Indications
- Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) or Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS)
- Severe hypoglycemia requiring IV dextrose
- Newly diagnosed Type 1 or insulin-requiring Type 2 with need for education
- Uncontrolled diabetes with acute illness or surgical planning

### Expected LOS
- Uncomplicated DKA: 3-5 days
- HHS: 5-7 days
- Severe hypoglycemia (no sequelae): 1-2 days
- Diabetes education/insulin initiation admission: 2-3 days

### Discharge Criteria
- Blood glucose stable: FPG < 180 mg/dL, no hypoglycemia for 24 hours
- Able to take oral medications or self-inject insulin
- DKA resolved: pH > 7.30, bicarbonate > 18, anion gap normalized, tolerating oral intake
- Patient/family education completed (SMBG technique, hypoglycemia management, sick-day rules)
- Follow-up appointment scheduled within 1-2 weeks

## 7. Referral Criteria

- **Endocrinologist**: Uncontrolled HbA1c > 9% despite dual/triple therapy, suspected Type 1/LADA, complex insulin regimens, recurrent DKA/HHS
- **Ophthalmologist**: At diagnosis for baseline, then annually; urgent if visual symptoms or proliferative retinopathy
- **Nephrologist**: eGFR < 30 mL/min, rapidly declining eGFR (> 5 mL/min/year), UACR > 300 mg/g despite RAAS blockade
- **Cardiologist**: Symptomatic coronary artery disease, heart failure, or abnormal cardiac stress test
- **Vascular surgeon**: Critical limb ischemia, non-healing diabetic foot ulcer with PAD
- **Podiatrist / Wound care**: Diabetic foot ulcer Wagner grade 2 or above
- **Dietitian**: All patients at diagnosis and annually; more frequent if not at target
- **Diabetes educator**: All patients; especially those initiating insulin
